# The-Effect-of-the-Affordable-Connectivity-Program-on-Households-

On November 15, 2021, the Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act (Infrastructure Act) became effective into law. The Infrastructure Act provided $14.2 billion into modifying and expanding the Emergency Broadband Benefit Program (EBB Program) to a longer-term affordability program, known as the Affordable Connectivity Program.  

The Affordable Connectivity Program is meant to address digital disparities due to Internet access being a necessity to every single person everywhere. There are two primary goals that ACP is meant to target: to increase the rate of broadband adoption and to make it easier to sustain in low-income households. This meant that qualifying low-income households would receive discounts on broadband services and connect devices. In terms of enrollment numbers and consumer benefits, 20 million total households enrolled nationwide (i.e. approximately 17 million households were in urban areas, whereas 3 million were in rural areas) and benefits included the following: 1,700 internet service providers; 8 million devices were distributed; and 46% of enrolled houses used ACP for fixed connection.
